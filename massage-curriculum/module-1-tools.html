Learning goals for Module 1

By the end of this module, students should be able to:

  • Describe the overall flow and expectations of the night class.
  • Identify the basic tools used in a massage treatment room and name their purpose.
  • Adjust table height for safe body mechanics for both therapist and client.
  • Explain at least three body mechanics principles they will apply during the back routine.

Course overview

Use this quick outline to ground students in the journey from first back routine through sports massage and business projects.

The course begins with tools, hygiene, and body mechanics, then moves into the back routine, full body flow, lymphatic drainage, clinical techniques, modalities, Asian bodywork, and business practices. Theory exams and practicums are woven throughout so students keep practicing what they are learning on the table.

  • Arrive prepared with required readings completed.
  • Come dressed and ready to give and receive bodywork.
  • Communicate clearly about injuries, pain, or limitations.
  • Practice safe draping and professional boundaries at all times.

Everything in this opening module becomes muscle memory later. How students set up a table, adjust height, use bolsters, and stand behind the table will either protect their bodies or wear them out. This foundation carries into every practicum and every client they touch.

Tools of the trade

Invite students to explore the core tools they will handle in the treatment room. They tap to flip each card for key teaching points.

Body mechanics quick check

Students read the scenario and choose the best body mechanics option. Feedback appears right under the question.

You are about to begin a basic back routine on a client who is taller than you. Which setup protects your body the most?

Reflection prompt

This gives students a place to connect the material to their own body and history with pain or fatigue.

Where in your own body do you already feel strain when you work or study, and what is one body mechanics habit you want to practice from this module to protect that area?

Next steps

Once students are comfortable with the room, tools, and basic body mechanics language, they are ready to move into the back routine module where they will start putting hands on and building flow.
